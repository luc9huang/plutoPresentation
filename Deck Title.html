Clyde Tombaugh discovered Pluto in 1930, by
using a device called the blink comparator.

The blink comparator is an optical instrument
used to detect small differences in two photographs
of the same field by viewing them alternately by
switching rapidly from one to the other.

Objects that are far outside our solar system,
such as stars and galaxies, will be in the same
place in the photographs.

Objects closer to Earth will appear to have moved.

Pluto's orbit around the Sun is also different from the planets' orbits in two ways.

1. The plane of Pluto's orbit is at a 17-degree angle to the plane of the planets' orbits.

2. For part of Pluto's orbit, Pluto is closer to the Sun than Neptune.
